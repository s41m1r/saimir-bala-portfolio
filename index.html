Bio and Research Profile

I am a Research Associate (PostDoc) at Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin, Germany and a WU PhD alumnus. My research lies at the crucial intersection of Process Mining, Business Analytics, and Software Engineering. I focus on developing methods that extract actionable, data-driven insights from complex process and behavioral data generated by software-intensive systems, bridging the gap between theoretical ML advances and real-world business decision challenges.

My experience spans international collaborations (e.g., WU Vienna, Weizenbaum-Institut, SAP Signavio) and substantial industry project involvement (Siemens, SAP Signavio, Phactum Softwareentwicklung). I am committed to advancing predictive and prescriptive analytics to improve system performance, resource allocation, and human decision-making in dynamic environments.

"My long-term research agenda aims to build an interdisciplinary group focused on predictive and prescriptive analytics for complex business and software processes, bridging data and process science with software engineering."

Core Research Areas

Process Mining & Analytics

Developing techniques to analyze semi-structured and object-centric event data (like software repositories) to reveal emergent behavioral patterns, assess quality attributes, and support decision-making in complex systems.

Predictive & Prescriptive Models

Building models for time-series forecasting, improving process outcomes, and deriving recommendations for resource allocation and system performance. Emphasis on empirical validation and interpretability (XAI).

Software Engineering

Focus on Lifecycle-Oriented Software Analytics, investigating long-running behaviors like architectural drift, incident response, and technical-debt evolution in multi-actor, continuous software processes.
